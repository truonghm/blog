This is the most confident I have ever felt in my 28 years of living. It's just mind-boggling to me how I can ever make it here, upto this moment, and not... die, but somehow I'm here and I feel grateful for that.

I see myself as a late-bloomer. It took me 6 years to finish college, and while many of my peers went on to get great job offers from multinational firms, I'm stuck with internships and an unfinished degree. I tried to take on as many opportunities as possible, thinking I was shooting for the moon. Many of them landed in garbage dumps, while only a few selected ones worked out, and even for those that did work out, I never managed to get the most out of them, mostly because I didn't know how to deal with people. Don't get me wrong, people still make me absolutely miserable, but at least now I know I can be useful to people, and as such I base all of my social interactions around that. **"Here's how I can be useful to you. You can choose to like me or not, and if you don't, fine. But if you do, great, we can be friends"** - that's my entire strategy for making friends. I understand it's not very healthy, but trust me I have tried other ways and it never worked. I can never talk to anyone and not feel like either they are going to hurt me, or I'm going to hurt them. However, I do like people, even as an introvert, as I occasionally enjoy being around people and observe them. And when I can be useful to others, it's with genuine intentions, because as long as I feel useful, I'm... fulfilled.

Two things make me happy: Learning and giving. Giving is an act of love. But love is never permanent, because it's a two-way street. Learning on the other hand is only for yourself, and learning always brings me so much joy. I know that I should have focused more on learning in high school and college, but I also try my best to tell myself that I regret nothing. I'm approaching 30 fast and it's a blessing to still be able to retain this much eagerness for learning. The thing about learning is that you can always give yourself more reason to learn, and it's entirely up to you when, where and how you learn. So in a way, you can say that I learn because I love myself and I want to give myself the best thing possible - knowledge.

When you look at life in such a condensed kind of way, you will see that most things in life are meaningless and should bear no influence on you. Most ideas are complete garbage, damaging even, and if an idea ever leads to a company being formed, 80% of employees of that company are not needed, and most jobs can be done with spreadsheets, or even better - text files, which are better off staying as pieces of paper whenever possible. And all paper is better off left as thoughts whenever possible, and most thoughts should be forsaken.

So yes, life is meaningless, but the act of pretending that it has meaning makes life meaningful, so you can do whatever you want, because literally nobody cares.
